Telegram is one of the world's largest messaging applications, used by hundreds of millions of adults for entirely legitimate personal, professional, and organisational communication. 🔒
Secret Chats — End-to-End Encrypted With Automatic Self-Destruct
Telegram's Secret Chat mode uses end-to-end encryption and stores messages only on the devices of the two participants — not on Telegram's servers. Within a Secret Chat, the sender can set a self-destruct timer ranging from one second to one week after which messages are automatically deleted from both devices. No record of the conversation remains on either device or any server once the timer expires. A child conducting conversations through Secret Chat mode can do so with complete confidence that no trace of the conversation will remain — not in the app history, not on Telegram's servers, and not on the recipient's device. This is a qualitatively different level of conversation privacy from standard message deletion, which typically leaves at least a record that messages existed.
📢
Public Channels With Minimal Content Moderation
Telegram allows any user to create a public channel — a broadcast platform that any other user can join and follow — with minimal content moderation compared to mainstream social media platforms. Public Telegram channels have been documented as hosting extremist content, illegal material, adult content, and other harmful information that would be removed from more moderated platforms. A child can search for and join any public channel, and Telegram's search function makes channels discoverable by keyword. The channel landscape on Telegram is significantly less moderated than the equivalent on Instagram, YouTube, or TikTok, and a child exploring public channels may encounter content that would not pass moderation on those platforms.
👤
Username-Based Contact Without Phone Number Sharing
Unlike WhatsApp, which requires a phone number to initiate contact, Telegram allows users to set a public username and be contacted by anyone who knows that username — without sharing their phone number. A child can share their Telegram username on social media, in gaming communities, or through any other channel, and anyone who encounters it can initiate a direct message without the child's phone number being exposed. Conversely, a child can be directed to contact an adult through their Telegram username without any phone number exchange. The username system extends Telegram's reach as a contact platform well beyond the child's existing phone contact list.
🤖
Bots and Inline Tools Can Deliver Content Outside Standard Moderation
Telegram's bot ecosystem allows third-party developers to create automated bots accessible within the app that can deliver content, media, and services directly through Telegram's interface. Bots on Telegram have been used to distribute content — including adult content and pirated material — that would be removed from mainstream app stores and platforms. A child directed to interact with a Telegram bot may receive content delivered through the bot's interface that bypasses the content moderation applied to standard Telegram channels and messages.
🌍
Cross-Platform and Fully Functional on Web — No App Required
Telegram is available on iOS, Android, Windows, macOS, and Linux, and has a fully functional web version at web.telegram.org. A child whose Telegram app is blocked on their phone can access their full Telegram account — including all chats, channels, and Secret Chats — through the web version in any browser, on any device. Restricting the Telegram app alone does not prevent Telegram access; DNS-level domain blocking for telegram.org and web.telegram.org is required for complete protection on home network-connected devices.

✈️
Secret Chats Are the Feature That Differentiates Telegram From All Other Standard Messaging Apps — No Other Major Consumer Messaging App Combines End-to-End Encryption With Automatic Message Deletion From Both Devices
WhatsApp has end-to-end encryption but does not automatically delete messages from both devices on a timer set by the sender. iMessage has end-to-end encryption and disappearing messages but the feature requires consent from both parties and is far less commonly used. Signal has both features but is primarily used by adults in privacy-conscious contexts and is not a mainstream teen social platform. Telegram's Secret Chat mode brings these capabilities together in a mainstream social platform with hundreds of millions of users and significant teenage adoption — making it the only major consumer messaging platform where a child can have completely untraceable conversations with automatic deletion that leaves no record anywhere. This specific capability is the primary parental oversight concern that distinguishes Telegram from other mainstream messaging platforms.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Telegram Is Mainstream and Legitimate — The Restriction Is About Specific Features, Not the Platform as a Whole
Telegram is used by hundreds of millions of adults worldwide for entirely legitimate communication — personal messaging, professional team communication, news channels, community groups, and many other benign purposes. A child who uses Telegram will correctly point out that it is a major mainstream platform and not an obscure or inherently risky app. The restriction is not about Telegram being primarily dangerous — it is about specific features that create parental oversight challenges significantly larger than those of standard messaging apps. A conversation about Telegram is most effective when it acknowledges the platform's legitimacy and focuses specifically on the features that differ from standard messaging apps — particularly Secret Chats with self-destruct timers and the public channel ecosystem — rather than presenting the platform itself as inherently harmful.
2
Secret Chats Are a Deliberate Choice — A Child Who Uses Them Has Specifically Chosen Untraceable Communication
Secret Chat mode is not the default on Telegram — standard Telegram messages are not end-to-end encrypted and are stored on Telegram's servers. A child who conducts conversations in Secret Chat mode has actively chosen to switch to that mode and has done so with the knowledge that the conversation will leave no trace. The self-destruct timer is set by the sender, not automatically. A child using Secret Chat mode has made a deliberate choice to have a conversation that will not be recoverable — which is a meaningfully different situation from standard messaging on any platform. A parent who finds Telegram on a child's device should ask specifically whether the child has been using Secret Chats — though there is no record to review if self-destruct timers have been active, because the conversations have already been automatically deleted.
3
Public Channels Are a Specific Risk — Telegram's Channel Ecosystem Is Less Moderated Than Mainstream Social Platforms
Telegram's public channel ecosystem is a separate risk dimension from its private messaging features. Public channels on Telegram have hosted content including extremist material, adult content, detailed instructions for harmful activities, and illegal material that would be removed from mainstream social platforms. A child who explores Telegram's public channel search can find channels on almost any topic — and the content moderation applied to those channels is significantly less rigorous than on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or Twitter. A parent who decides to allow Telegram should specifically discuss the public channel ecosystem with the child and set explicit expectations about which types of channels are appropriate — not assuming that the channel content is equivalent to content on more moderated platforms.
4
Blocking the App Is Not Sufficient — The Web Version Provides Full Access Through Any Browser
Unlike most apps in this document, blocking the Telegram mobile app does not prevent Telegram access. The full Telegram experience — including all private chats, Secret Chats, channels, and bots — is accessible at web.telegram.org in any standard browser on any device. A child whose Telegram app is restricted can open any browser, navigate to web.telegram.org, log in with their account credentials, and access their full Telegram account including all conversations. Complete protection requires DNS-level domain blocking for telegram.org and its subdomains on the home network in addition to the app-level restriction. For devices using mobile data outside the home network, the domain block does not apply — meaning a child on mobile data outside the home can still access the web version unless device-level DNS filtering is also applied.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps that reflect Telegram's specific profile — acknowledging its legitimacy while addressing the features that differ from standard messaging apps, applying both app and web-level restrictions, and having a feature-specific conversation rather than a general platform conversation.

💬
Address the Specific Features — Not Telegram as a Whole
The most effective conversation about Telegram focuses on its specific differentiating features rather than the platform as a whole — because a child will correctly identify Telegram as a major mainstream platform and will resist a conversation that treats it as inherently dangerous. Acknowledge that Telegram is a widely used legitimate platform, then explain the specific features that are different from standard messaging apps: Secret Chat mode with self-destruct timers that leave no record anywhere, public channels with less moderation than mainstream social platforms, and username-based contact that extends beyond the phone contact list. For each feature, explain specifically what the oversight gap is — not as a parental control concern but as a practical question of what the feature enables: does the child understand that Secret Chats leave no trace on any device or server? Does the child understand that public channel content is less moderated than what they see on Instagram or YouTube? These specific feature discussions are more productive than a general conversation about platform safety.
🔎
If Telegram Is Already on the Device — Check Joined Channels and Recent Chats Before Restricting
If Telegram is found on a child's device, review the app's content before applying a restriction. The list of channels the child has joined is visible in the left sidebar — checking for any channels whose names or descriptions suggest harmful content gives important context. Also review the recent chat list for any contacts who are not in the child's phone contact list — contacts reached through Telegram usernames rather than phone numbers are a signal that the child has been connecting with people outside their established social circle. Note that if Secret Chats with self-destruct timers have been in use, their content will not be visible — you may see that a Secret Chat exists with a contact without being able to read what was in it if messages have self-destructed. The presence of Secret Chats with unfamiliar contacts is itself meaningful context for the conversation, even without access to the content.
🔒
Apply Both the iLimits App Restriction and DNS-Level Blocking for telegram.org
Apply the iLimits restriction to block the Telegram app on the child's device, and add telegram.org and web.telegram.org to any DNS or router-level domain blocklist on the home network. Both steps are needed — the app restriction prevents mobile app use, and the DNS block prevents access through any browser on any home-network device. For children with mobile data access, DNS filtering on the device itself — through a mobile DNS filtering service — extends this protection to sessions outside the home network. If the child has a Telegram account and the decision is made to restrict it rather than simply prevent initial installation, also consider whether the child should delete the account itself — a Telegram account can be accessed from any browser worldwide regardless of app restrictions, and account deletion is the only complete closure if the concern extends beyond the home network. Account deletion is done through Telegram's settings under Privacy and Security and Delete Account.
🔐

A Mainstream Platform With Non-Mainstream Features — The Features Are What Matter

Telegram is a legitimate platform used by hundreds of millions of people — and most of them use it for entirely ordinary communication. The restriction is not about what Telegram primarily is. It is about what Secret Chat mode with self-destruct timers specifically enables, about what public channels with minimal moderation specifically expose children to, and about what username-based contact without phone number sharing specifically allows. iLimits blocks the app. DNS filtering closes the web version. The conversation explains the features — so that a child who understands what Secret Chat mode actually does can make an informed choice about whether to use it, rather than simply knowing that Telegram has been restricted.
