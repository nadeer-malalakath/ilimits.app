📱 Section 1

What This Platform Does

A clear picture of Amino Apps — a network of thousands of fan communities built around shared interests such as anime, gaming, K-pop and creative writing, where users can post content, chat publicly and privately, and interact freely with any other member regardless of age.

🧬
Amino Is a Network of Thousands of Fan Communities — Many Entirely Unmoderated and Accessible to Children and Adults Side by Side
Amino Apps is a social platform built around fandom and interest-based communities called Aminos. Each Amino is dedicated to a specific topic — anime, gaming, K-pop, creative writing, and thousands more — and users can join any Amino, post content, comment, engage in public chats and send private direct messages to any other member. The platform is extremely popular with children and teenagers, particularly those interested in anime and gaming culture. However, the same communities that attract children also contain adult users, and the platform's moderation across its thousands of individual communities is inconsistent at best. Adult-oriented fan content, explicit creative writing and direct messaging with strangers are all standard features of the platform that children can access freely.

Amino Apps is a network of fan and interest communities where users post content, participate in group chats and send private messages to other members. With a user base that skews heavily young and communities built around topics children are passionate about — anime, gaming, K-pop — the platform's reach into children's digital lives is significant. 🌐
Thousands of Individual Fan Communities Across Every Interest Category
Amino hosts thousands of separate community spaces — each dedicated to a specific fandom or interest and each operated by community leaders who set their own rules and moderation standards. The quality and safety of moderation varies enormously between communities, and many communities have no active moderation at all. A child joining an Amino dedicated to their favourite anime or game is joining a community that may contain adult users, adult-themed fan content and private messaging access to every other member — all within an app that presents itself as a safe, interest-based social space.
💌
Private Direct Messaging Between Any Two Members on the Platform
Any two members of Amino can send each other private direct messages, regardless of which communities they share or whether they have interacted before. This means that an adult who sees a child's public post or profile in any Amino community can initiate a private conversation with that child with no friction. Direct messages are private and not visible to parents, community moderators or Amino staff unless reported. The platform has been cited in multiple safeguarding investigations as a channel through which adults initiated contact with children before progressing to exploitative conversations.
✍️
Fan Fiction and Creative Writing Including Explicit Adult Content
Amino communities regularly host user-generated fan fiction and creative writing, much of which involves characters from popular anime, gaming and entertainment franchises. A significant proportion of this content is sexually explicit, depicting characters — including characters who are children in their source material — in adult scenarios. While Amino has policies against explicit content, enforcement is inconsistent across thousands of communities, and explicit fan fiction and artwork are regularly accessible in communities that children join for entirely innocent reasons related to their fandom interests.
💬
Open Group Chats With No Verified Age Separation
Amino's group chat feature allows any community members to participate in real-time text conversations with an unlimited number of other users. These chats are not age-separated and are open to any member of the community. A child participating in a group chat about their favourite anime series may be sharing that space with adults who are strangers, with no indication of participants' ages and no safeguard preventing adults from engaging directly with children in the chat. Group chats can also be used to share images and links, including content that would not be appropriate for children.
🖼️
User-Generated Image and Blog Posts With Inconsistent Content Moderation
Members post images, artwork, blog posts and other content freely within Amino communities. Content moderation relies primarily on community leaders and user reports rather than proactive review by Amino itself, and the volume of content posted across the platform means that explicit or inappropriate material regularly appears in communities before it is reported and removed. A child browsing posts in a community they have joined for its fandom content may encounter adult artwork or explicit posts as part of a normal browsing session, without having searched for such material.

👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Amino Looks Like a Safe Fandom Space — It Is Not Moderated or Designed With Child Safety as a Priority
Amino's communities are built around topics that children love — anime, gaming, K-pop, creative fandoms — and the platform presents itself as a welcoming, interest-based social space. This can make it feel, to both children and parents, like a safe and positive place to connect with others who share their interests. In practice, the platform operates thousands of communities that it cannot consistently moderate, contains explicit adult content that circulates regularly in communities children join, and provides direct messaging access that any adult can use to contact any child whose profile they encounter. The platform's appeal to children is precisely what makes it a risk — and precisely what makes adults who seek contact with children aware of it as a space where young people are reliably present.
2
Explicit Fan Content in Anime and Gaming Communities Is a Specific and Documented Risk on Amino
Fan communities around anime and gaming are among the most active on Amino — and also among the most likely to contain explicit user-generated content, including sexually explicit fan fiction and artwork. Some of this content depicts characters who are children or teenagers in their source material. A child who joins an Amino community for their favourite anime series may encounter explicit content about characters they recognise and like, posted by other members of the community, without having searched for such material and without any warning. This is not a theoretical risk — it is a documented and widely reported feature of these communities that Amino has not been able to address systematically across its platform.
3
Children May Not Tell Parents They Are Using Amino Because It Feels Like a Normal Fandom Activity
Because Amino is organised around fandom interests rather than social networking in an obvious sense, children may not think of it as a social media platform in the way they think of Instagram or TikTok — and may therefore not mention it to parents when discussing their online activities. A child who uses Amino to participate in fan communities about their interests may genuinely not frame this as using social media, even though the platform provides all the same contact and content exposure risks. Parents should be aware that Amino may be in use on a child's device without the child having thought to mention it, and should check for the app as part of any regular device review.
4
The Platform's Community Structure Makes It Difficult to Restrict by Topic — Platform-Level Blocking Is More Reliable
Unlike platforms with a single feed or content stream, Amino's risk is distributed across thousands of individual communities — meaning that a parent cannot simply identify one or two risky communities to avoid and consider their child protected. A child who joins ten different Amino communities related to their interests may encounter explicit content or adult contact in any one of them, depending on which communities happen to have active bad actors or poor moderation at any given time. Platform-level blocking through iLimits — rather than attempting to manage community-by-community access — is therefore the more reliable approach to reducing the risks Amino presents for children.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Practical steps to protect your child from Amino's risks while acknowledging the genuine appeal of fandom communities — and how to support your child's interest in their fandoms through safer alternatives.

💬
Talk With Your Child About Why Fandom Spaces Can Still Be Risky
Children who use Amino are typically doing so because they love a fandom — an anime series, a game, a music group — and want to share that interest with others who feel the same way. Acknowledging this genuinely positive motivation, while explaining clearly why a platform like Amino carries real risks that are not obvious from its surface appearance, is the most effective way to help your child understand why you are restricting access. A child who understands that the risk comes not from the fandom itself but from a platform that gives adults access to children without meaningful safeguards — and that better alternatives exist — is more likely to accept a restriction and less likely to attempt to circumvent it.
🌱
Help Your Child Find Safer Ways to Engage With Their Fandom Interests
Restricting Amino is more likely to be accepted — and more beneficial for your child's long-term digital habits — if you take the time to identify safer alternatives for their specific fandom interests. Moderated fan wikis, official community spaces on major platforms with stronger safety records, and fan communities on platforms with better parental control tools all provide ways for children to engage with the things they love without the contact and content risks that Amino presents. Helping your child find these alternatives communicates that you are not trying to restrict their interests, only to ensure they engage with them safely.
🔒
Check Your Child's Devices for Amino and Apply iLimits Controls Across All of Them
Because Amino may be in use on a child's device without parents being aware of it, check your child's phone, tablet and any shared household devices for the Amino app as part of any regular device review. Apply iLimits restrictions at both the app and browser level across every device your child has access to, and review those restrictions when your child acquires a new device or begins using a new network. The combination of technical controls through iLimits and an ongoing, open conversation about safe fandom engagement gives your family the most effective and durable protection available.
🔐

A Child's Passions Deserve Safe Spaces — Make Sure the Spaces They Use Actually Are

The fandoms your child loves are a genuine and valuable part of who they are — and they deserve to explore them in spaces that are actually safe. Amino's surface appeal as a fandom community platform conceals risks that are real, documented and difficult to mitigate through conversation alone. iLimits gives you the tools to act on those risks, and the conversations you have with your child about safe fandom engagement help them carry those habits into every online space they use.
