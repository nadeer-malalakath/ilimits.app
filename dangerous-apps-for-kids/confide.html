What This App Does

A clear and honest picture of Confide — an encrypted self-destructing messaging app with built-in screenshot prevention, designed for confidential adult professional communication, carrying the same core risks as Wickr Me and adding one specific additional feature that removes the primary evidence-preservation method available when harmful contact is discovered on a child's device.

🤫
Confide Sends Self-Destructing Encrypted Messages That Cannot Be Screenshotted — Making It Harder to Preserve Evidence of Harmful Contact Than Any Other App in This Category
Confide is an encrypted messaging app specifically designed for confidential professional communication. Messages are end-to-end encrypted, delete automatically after being read, and — uniquely — reveal text only one line at a time while actively preventing screenshots through technical means. For an adult who wants to contact a child without any recoverable record, Confide removes not just the message history but also the ability to preserve that history through screenshots — the primary method parents and law enforcement use when harmful contact is discovered on a device. This specific additional feature is what distinguishes Confide from Wickr Me in a child safety context.

🔐
End-to-End Encrypted Messages That Delete After Being Read
Confide's messages are end-to-end encrypted and automatically delete after the recipient reads them — leaving no conversation history on either device. Like Wickr Me, this means a parent who discovers Confide on a child's device and opens the app finds no recoverable message history. The content of communications through Confide is unavailable to anyone except the direct participants while the messages are active, and unavailable to anyone at all once they have been read and deleted.
📵
Built-In Screenshot Prevention — Blocks the Primary Evidence Preservation Method
Confide actively prevents screenshots of message content — it detects screenshot attempts and blocks them at the system level, and reveals message text only one line at a time to further prevent capture of the full message in a single image. This is the specific additional feature that distinguishes Confide from Wickr Me for child safety purposes: on Wickr Me, a parent who discovers a conversation before deletion timers run can screenshot visible content as evidence. On Confide, this emergency evidence preservation method is technically blocked by design. An adult who communicates with a child through Confide has specifically chosen a tool that prevents even partial evidence preservation.
👤
Requires an Email Address — Partial Identity, Not Full Anonymity
Unlike Wickr Me, Confide requires an email address to create an account. This provides partial identity verification — a real email address is associated with the account. However, email accounts are trivially easy to create with false information, and an adult who specifically wants unaccountable contact with a child can create a disposable email account for the purpose. The email requirement provides marginally more potential identification than Wickr Me's fully anonymous accounts, but does not meaningfully prevent an adult from using Confide to contact a child anonymously in practice.
💼
Professional-Focused Marketing — May Appear More Legitimate Than Other Encrypted Apps
Confide markets itself primarily to business and professional users as a tool for confidential workplace communication — protecting sensitive discussions from leaking. This professional framing may make it appear more legitimate and less concerning than apps with more obviously consumer-facing privacy marketing. A child who has Confide installed, or who has been directed to install it by an adult contact, may present it as a professional tool used by grown-ups — framing that does not change the app's capabilities or the concern its presence on a child's device creates.
📞
Encrypted Voice Calls in Addition to Text
Confide supports encrypted voice calls alongside text messaging. Like all content on the platform, these calls are encrypted and leave no recoverable record. An adult who contacts a child through Confide can conduct voice calls that create no accessible evidence of the conversation's content, timing or duration.

What Parents Should Know

1
Confide Has the Same Core Risks as Wickr Me — With One Specific Feature That Makes It More Difficult to Investigate
Parents who have read the Wickr Me page will recognise the same pattern: encrypted content, automatic deletion, a channel specifically suited to unaccountable adult contact with children. Confide adds one specific feature on top: built-in screenshot prevention. On Wickr Me, a parent who discovers the app while messages are still visible has a window to capture evidence. Confide closes that window by design. This makes Confide the most complete evidence-elimination messaging tool in consumer use, and an adult who chooses it specifically to contact a child has made a more deliberate choice about evidence avoidance than one who uses Wickr Me. Both apps should be restricted — Confide's additional feature makes it a higher evidence-preservation concern, not a categorically different risk.
2
Confide's Professional Framing Does Not Change Its Risk Profile on a Child's Device
Confide's marketing as a professional confidentiality tool — used by businesses and individuals for sensitive workplace communications — may cause parents to view it as a legitimate productivity app rather than a potential child safety concern. The professional framing is accurate for Confide's primary use case among adults, and does not indicate that a child's installation is necessarily harmful. What the professional framing does not change is the app's technical capabilities: self-destructing messages, end-to-end encryption, and screenshot prevention are present regardless of the context in which the app is used, and those features create the same risk profile on a child's device whether the app is marketed to businesses or teenagers.
3
Finding Confide Warrants the Same Investigation as Finding Wickr Me — Including Checking Other Platforms
Like Wickr Me, Confide is rarely the first platform through which an adult makes contact with a child — it is typically where contact moves after an initial relationship has been established elsewhere. A child who has Confide installed may have been directed there by a contact from a gaming platform, social media, a random chat service, or another context. The investigation should cover not just Confide but wherever the original contact was made. Unlike Wickr Me, where visible message content can sometimes be preserved through screenshots, Confide's screenshot prevention means there may be no recoverable evidence from the app itself regardless of when it is discovered. The investigation must rely primarily on what the child can recall and disclose, and the conversation approach described in the Wickr Me guidance applies here in full.

Confide and Wickr Me are separate apps with separate listings that must be restricted independently. A child who has been restricted from Wickr Me may use Confide as an alternative — possibly because a contact directed them there, or because they sought a similar tool. Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps for Confide — noting the screenshot prevention difference from Wickr Me, how to investigate without in-app evidence, and how category-level blocking covers both apps together.

📵
Unlike Wickr Me — You Cannot Screenshot Confide Content as Evidence
The Wickr Me page advises parents to screenshot any visible message content immediately when the app is found on a device. This step is not available with Confide — its screenshot prevention technology blocks this emergency evidence preservation at the system level, and its line-by-line text display further prevents full message capture. If Confide is found on a child's device and there is reason to believe contact with an unknown adult has occurred, the investigation must rely on: the child's own account of what contact took place and who it was with; any metadata visible within the app (contact usernames, conversation counts, recent activity indicators) that does not involve message content; and investigation of other platforms where the original contact may have been established. If you believe harmful contact has occurred, contact the relevant platform through Confide's reporting mechanisms and, if the situation warrants it, law enforcement — who may have access to investigation methods beyond those available to parents.
🔍
Investigate Other Platforms for the Contact That Led to Confide — the Evidence Is There
Because Confide prevents in-app evidence preservation, the investigation of how Confide came to be on the device and who directed the child there becomes more important than with Wickr Me. Check the child's other messaging apps, gaming platforms and social accounts for contacts that do not belong to their known social circle, and look for any conversation that references Confide or suggests moving to a different, more private channel. The platform where initial contact was established will typically have more recoverable evidence than Confide itself — follow the chain of contact backwards from the Confide installation to find the original platform, preserve whatever is available there, and build the picture of who has been in contact with your child from the platforms where evidence is accessible rather than from Confide where it is not.
