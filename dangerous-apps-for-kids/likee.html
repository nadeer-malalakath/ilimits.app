What This Platform Does

A clear picture of Likee — a short-form video creation and sharing app that allows users to record, edit and publish videos with special effects, and which exposes viewers to an algorithmically curated feed of content from strangers, including content that is frequently sexualised or age-inappropriate.

⚠️
Likee Has Been the Subject of Multiple Child Safety Investigations and Regulatory Actions in Several Countries — Its Risks Are Well-Documented and Serious
Likee is a short-form video platform developed by BIGO Technology and primarily popular in Southeast Asia, the Middle East and parts of Europe and Africa. Despite presenting itself as a creative video platform for general audiences, Likee has been the subject of child safety investigations and regulatory scrutiny in multiple countries, including findings related to sexually suggestive content served to minors, insufficient age verification, adult access to children's accounts, and documented grooming incidents. The platform's algorithmic content feed, public-by-default profile settings and weak moderation make it a high-risk environment for children of all ages.

Likee is a short-form video creation and discovery platform where users record and publish videos using the app's built-in effects, filters and editing tools, and where other users discover that content through an algorithmically curated feed that serves videos from strangers based on engagement patterns rather than followed accounts — a structure that gives the platform's algorithm, rather than the user, control over what content a child sees.
🎞️
Algorithmically Curated Video Feed Served From Strangers' Accounts
Likee's primary content experience is an algorithmically curated feed of short videos from accounts the user does not follow, similar in structure to TikTok's For You Page. This means a child opening the app is immediately served content from strangers, with the algorithm determining what is shown based on engagement signals. Because the algorithm optimises for watch time rather than age-appropriateness, children are routinely served content that includes sexualised dancing, adult humour and other material that would not pass moderation on a platform with robust content controls. Parents cannot predict or control what content the algorithm will serve to their child's account.
👤
Public-by-Default Profiles Visible to All Users Including Adults
Likee accounts are public by default, meaning a child's profile — including their published videos, username, follower list and the videos they have interacted with — is visible to any user on the platform, including adults with no connection to the child. Public profiles allow unknown adults to identify, follow and directly engage with children's content before any contact is initiated. While privacy settings exist, they are not applied automatically to new accounts, and children who set up accounts independently are unlikely to locate and apply them. The platform's default openness is a structural risk factor that creates the conditions for grooming and unwanted adult contact.
💬
Direct Messaging Between Users Including Unconnected Adults and Children
Likee allows direct messaging between users who have not established a prior connection, meaning unknown adults can send private messages directly to a child's account without any mutual follow or connection requirement. Direct messaging is one of the primary channels through which grooming contact is initiated on social video platforms — the combination of public profiles that allow adults to identify child accounts and an open messaging system that allows unsolicited contact creates the conditions that have contributed to documented child safety incidents on the platform.
📡
Live Streaming With Real-Money Virtual Gifting
Likee includes a live streaming feature through which users broadcast themselves in real time to followers and other platform users. Live streams on Likee frequently feature content that is inappropriate for children — including sexualised performance, adult behaviour and unmoderated commentary from viewers. The platform's live gifting system allows viewers to send virtual gifts purchased with real money, incentivising broadcasters to produce content that maximises viewer engagement and spending. Children who watch live streams are exposed both to the content itself and to the social dynamics of a gifting economy that encourages financial participation.
🔍
No Reliable Age Verification and Weak Content Moderation
Likee does not apply meaningful age verification at account creation — users provide a date of birth, but no identity check is performed to verify the information provided. The platform's content moderation has been criticised in multiple investigations for failing to remove sexualised content involving minors and for allowing adult users to maintain open access to children's accounts. Regulatory investigations in several countries have found that Likee's moderation and safety systems are inadequate relative to the risks posed by its open, algorithm-driven design — and the platform's improvements in response to regulatory pressure have been inconsistent across its different regional versions.

What Parents Should Know

1
Likee's Risks Are Documented — Not Just Theoretical
Many social platform risks are discussed in general terms as possibilities — potential exposure to inappropriate content, potential contact from strangers. Likee is different in that its risks have been documented through formal regulatory investigations and published child safety findings in multiple countries. Parents who are deciding whether to restrict the platform do not need to extrapolate from general principles — they can be confident that the risks associated with Likee are established, serious and ongoing. The platform has not, at the time of this writing, made systemic changes sufficient to resolve the concerns raised by regulatory investigators.
2
The Platform's Appeal to Young Children Is a Significant Concern
Likee's video creation tools — special effects, face filters, animated stickers and music integration — have strong appeal to younger children, including primary school-age children who may discover the platform through friends or siblings. The gap between the age at which children are drawn to Likee's creative features and the age at which the platform's safety systems might be considered adequate is a central concern. Parents of younger children in particular should be alert to the possibility that their child has discovered or is using the platform without parental awareness, as the app's colourful, creative interface does not signal the risks it carries to an uninformed viewer.
3
Children May Have Active Likee Accounts Parents Are Not Aware Of
Because Likee requires only a date of birth at registration — which any child can falsify — and because the app's interface does not look obviously risky to a child or a parent who does not know what to look for, children may have established active accounts without their parents' knowledge. If a parent is not certain whether their child has a Likee account, a direct and calm conversation is more likely to surface an existing account than a device search alone. Parents who discover an existing account should review the account's privacy settings, check the direct message history and consider together with their child whether continued use is appropriate and under what conditions.
4
Privacy Settings Exist but Are Not Applied by Default and Must Be Actively Configured
Likee does offer privacy settings that can make an account private, restrict direct messages and limit who can comment on a child's videos. However, these settings are not applied automatically to new accounts and must be located and configured manually. Children who set up accounts independently — which is the majority of children on the platform — will not have applied these settings. If a parent decides that supervised use of Likee is appropriate for their child rather than full restriction, configuring the account's privacy settings to the most restrictive available options, removing the direct messaging feature, and ensuring the account does not display the child's real name or location is the minimum baseline before use begins.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from the documented risks associated with Likee, and to build the open conversation and digital literacy that help children recognise and respond to unsafe situations on social platforms.

💬
Have a Direct Conversation About Stranger Contact and Grooming on Social Platforms
Given the documented grooming-related incidents associated with Likee specifically, having a direct and age-appropriate conversation with your child about what grooming looks like on social platforms — how it typically starts, why an adult might seek to make a child feel special or to build a private relationship online, and what to do if they experience it — is one of the most important protective steps a parent can take. This conversation does not need to be alarming or clinical; it can be framed around helping your child recognise when someone online is not who they say they are, and reinforcing that coming to you immediately in that situation is always the right response, without consequences for the child. Children who have had this conversation are significantly better equipped to recognise and exit grooming contact than those who have not.
🔎
Check Whether Your Child Already Has a Likee Account
Because Likee has strong appeal to younger children and requires only a falsifiable date of birth to register, many children have active Likee accounts that their parents are not aware of. Before applying a restriction, it is worth having a calm, non-accusatory conversation with your child about whether they use Likee — making clear that the conversation is about keeping them safe rather than getting them in trouble. If your child does have an account, reviewing it together — looking at privacy settings, direct message history and who follows them — gives you a clear picture of any existing risk and an opportunity to discuss why the platform is being restricted. Addressing an existing account alongside applying a technical restriction is more complete than applying the restriction alone.
🔒
Apply iLimits Restrictions Across All Devices and All Access Routes
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child has access to — including tablets, secondary phones and shared family computers — and ensure that browser-based access to the platform is also addressed, since Likee content is accessible through a web browser without downloading the app. If your child uses a school device or accesses the internet through a network you do not control, consider discussing the platform restriction with them directly so they understand why it exists and are less likely to use uncontrolled devices or networks to circumvent it. Technical controls and open conversation together provide the most durable and complete protection for your child against the documented risks associated with this platform.
