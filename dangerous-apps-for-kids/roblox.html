📱 Section 1

What This Platform Does

A clear picture of Roblox — an online gaming and social platform that allows users to play, create and share games with others worldwide, with a user base that is predominantly made up of children and teenagers.

🎮
Roblox Is a Gaming and Social Platform Used by Millions of Children — With Features That Require Active Parental Awareness
Roblox is one of the world's most widely used online platforms among children and teenagers, with hundreds of millions of registered accounts and a large proportion of its active user base under the age of 13. The platform combines gameplay, social interaction, user-generated content creation and a virtual economy built around its in-game currency, Robux. While Roblox provides parental control settings and content moderation tools, the open and social nature of the platform means that active parental engagement — including awareness of who children are communicating with, what content they are accessing and how much they are spending — is essential to safe use.

Roblox is a user-generated gaming and social platform where players can explore millions of games created by other users, interact through live and asynchronous chat, build and publish their own games and experiences, and participate in a virtual economy using Robux — a purchased in-game currency. The platform is accessible via app on iOS, Android and gaming consoles, and through any web browser.
🕹️
Millions of User-Created Games Across All Themes and Genres
Roblox hosts an enormous library of games and interactive experiences created by its own users — ranging from age-appropriate games designed for younger children to experiences that simulate violence, horror, or themes that may not be suitable for all age groups. Because the content library is user-generated and extremely large, content moderation cannot guarantee that all available experiences are appropriate for every age. Parents should be aware of which games their child is playing within Roblox, not only that they are using the platform in general.
💬
Live and In-Game Chat With Other Users
Roblox includes live chat functionality within games and experiences, allowing players to communicate with other users in real time. The platform applies automated chat filters — particularly for accounts registered as under-13 — that attempt to block personally identifiable information and inappropriate language. However, no automated moderation system is entirely reliable, and children can encounter attempts to move conversations to unmonitored external platforms. Parents should discuss online communication safety with children using Roblox.
💰
In-App Currency (Robux) and Purchasing System
Roblox operates an in-game currency system called Robux, which is purchased with real money and used to buy avatar items, game passes and access to premium experiences. The purchasing system is integrated into the platform's normal flow and can be accessed directly from a child's device if a parent's payment method is stored. Without spending controls in place, children can make repeated purchases that accumulate into significant real-world costs. Roblox offers parental spending controls, but these must be actively configured by the parent.
🌐
Social Features Including Friend Lists and Direct Messaging
Beyond in-game chat, Roblox includes friend request systems, follower features and, on some account types, direct messaging between users. These social features extend the platform beyond a simple gaming application and into the territory of a social network — with the associated risks of contact from unknown adults, social pressures around friend counts and status, and potential exposure to content shared through social channels within the platform.
🛠️
Game Creation and Publishing Tools Available to All Users
Roblox Studio — the platform's game development tool — is available to all users, including children, and allows them to create and publish their own games and experiences for other players. While this is a genuinely valuable creative tool, it also means that children are not only consumers of content on the platform but potential publishers of it. Parents should be aware that their child may be building and sharing content with the platform's global user base, not only playing games created by others.
⚠️ Section 2

Why It Requires Parental Awareness

Key risks associated with Roblox use by children, including exposure to inappropriate user-generated content, contact risks from unknown adults, in-app spending, and the social pressures inherent in a platform with hundreds of millions of users.

🎭
User-Generated Content Varies Widely in Suitability
Because Roblox's game library is user-created, the content available spans a very wide range of themes — including games simulating violence, horror, or adult social scenarios that may not be appropriate for younger children despite the platform's overall child-friendly positioning
🧑‍💻
Contact Risk From Unknown Adults
Roblox's open social features mean that children can receive friend requests from and communicate with unknown adults. The platform's large size and predominantly young user base can make it a target for adults seeking to contact children, including attempts to move conversations off-platform
💸
In-App Spending Can Escalate Quickly
The Robux purchasing system is designed to be frictionless and is integrated throughout the platform experience. Without active spending controls, children may make multiple small purchases that accumulate into significant amounts — often without a clear sense of the real-world value being spent
⏱️
Highly Engaging Design Encourages Extended Use
Roblox is designed to be deeply engaging, with social elements, regular updates and an enormous content library that can make it difficult for children to disengage voluntarily. Extended daily use — particularly at the expense of sleep, homework or physical activity — is a common concern reported by parents
👥
Social Pressures Around Status and In-Game Items
As a platform with a large social dimension, Roblox can generate social pressures around avatar appearance, item ownership and popularity. Children may feel pressure to spend Robux to keep up with peers, or experience social exclusion within the platform's community dynamics
🔇
Chat Filters Are Not Fully Reliable
While Roblox applies automated chat filtering — particularly on under-13 accounts — no automated system catches all inappropriate communication. Children can be exposed to offensive language, attempts to share external contact details, or content designed to circumvent filters through coded language or deliberate misspellings
🟡
Roblox Carries a Moderate Risk Rating — Safe With Active Parental Involvement Roblox is a legitimate and popular platform that many children enjoy safely, but its open social features, user-generated content library and integrated spending system mean that safe use requires active parental engagement, configured account controls, and regular conversation with children about their activity on the platform.
⚙️
Roblox Provides Parental Control Settings — But They Must Be Actively Configured and Are Not Enabled by Default
Roblox offers a parental controls system called Parental Controls, accessible through the platform's settings, which allows parents to restrict chat, limit spending, set account restrictions that limit access to age-rated content, and receive a monthly summary of their child's activity. These controls are not enabled by default and require a parent to actively create a parent account and link it to their child's account. Parents should configure these settings as a first step, then use iLimits to add an additional layer of oversight and screen time management on top of the platform's own tools.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Roblox Is Primarily a Children's Platform, But Its Open Nature Introduces Real Risks That Require Active Management
Roblox is not an adult platform — it is designed for and predominantly used by children, and the company invests significantly in moderation and child safety features. However, the platform's enormous scale, open social features and user-generated content mean that risks exist that cannot be entirely eliminated by the platform itself. Parents who treat Roblox as a sealed, child-safe environment without any oversight are likely to be unaware of their child's actual experience on the platform. Understanding what Roblox contains and how its features work is the foundation of keeping a child safe while using it.
2
Configure the Platform's Built-In Parental Controls Before Allowing Access
Roblox's parental control features — including chat restrictions, account restrictions that limit exposure to age-rated content, and spending controls — are not active by default and must be configured by a parent. Creating a linked parent account through Roblox's family settings is the single most important practical step a parent can take before their child begins using the platform. Without these settings in place, a child's account operates with default settings that are more permissive than most parents would choose if they were aware of the options available.
3
Talk to Children About the Specific Risks Associated With Online Gaming Platforms
Children using Roblox benefit from age-appropriate conversations about the risks specific to online gaming platforms — including the possibility of contact from unknown adults, the importance of never sharing personal information in chat, the real-world value of in-game purchases, and the importance of telling a parent immediately if something makes them uncomfortable. These conversations are more effective when they are specific and practical rather than general warnings about internet safety, and should be revisited periodically as children's use of the platform develops.
4
Set and Regularly Review Screen Time Limits for Platform Use
Roblox is designed to be engaging and can absorb very large amounts of a child's time without the child having a clear sense of how long they have been using it. Setting clear, consistently enforced time limits — and using iLimits to apply those limits technically rather than relying on self-regulation alone — is one of the most practical things parents can do to ensure that platform use remains a healthy part of a child's day rather than displacing sleep, homework, physical activity or face-to-face social interaction.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to help your child enjoy Roblox safely, with the right boundaries, the right conversations and the right technical controls in place.

💬
Talk Openly With Children About Online Gaming Safety
Children who understand the specific risks of online gaming platforms — including the possibility of contact from unknown adults, the real-world cost of in-game purchases, and the importance of not sharing personal information in chat — are significantly better equipped to respond appropriately when they encounter these situations. The conversation does not need to be alarming or extensive; it needs to be practical, specific to the platform they are using, and delivered in a way that makes it clear they can come to a parent without fear if something concerns them. Regular brief check-ins about what they are playing and who they are talking to are more effective than a single detailed conversation at the start of use.
⚙️
Configure Roblox's Parental Controls Before Your Child Begins Using the Platform
Roblox's built-in parental controls — accessed by creating a linked parent account through the platform's family settings — allow parents to restrict chat to approved contacts, limit account access to age-appropriate content, control spending and receive activity summaries. These settings represent the most direct layer of protection available within the platform and should be configured as a first step, before a child begins using their account. Review the settings periodically, as the platform updates its features and controls regularly, and what was configured at account creation may not reflect all available options.
⏱️
Set Clear Time Limits and Use Technical Controls to Enforce Them
Self-regulation of screen time is genuinely difficult for children when using an engaging platform like Roblox — the platform's design actively encourages continued engagement. Setting clear household rules about how much time can be spent on the platform each day, and using iLimits to enforce those limits technically rather than relying on a child to stop voluntarily, removes the need for repeated negotiations and reduces conflict around screen time. Combine technical time limits with consistent household routines — no Roblox before homework is done, no Roblox after a set evening time — to create a sustainable, balanced approach to the platform.
