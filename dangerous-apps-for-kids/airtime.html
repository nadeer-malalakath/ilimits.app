📱 Section 1

What This Platform Does

A clear picture of Airtime — a social platform that lets users watch videos, listen to music and share content together in real time while communicating via live video, voice and text chat, often alongside or with strangers in open public rooms.

📺
Airtime Is Built Around Real-Time Shared Experiences With Strangers — Including Live Video Chat in Open Public Rooms
Airtime is a social platform designed for co-watching and co-listening experiences, where users join rooms to watch videos or listen to music together while chatting via text, voice or live video. The platform's central feature — the ability to join public rooms and interact with other users in real time — means that a child using Airtime may be in live video or voice contact with adult strangers immediately upon opening the app. Rooms are discoverable by anyone on the platform, and content shared within rooms is not subject to meaningful pre-moderation. The app has been popular with teenagers, which also makes it a platform that adults may use specifically to seek contact with younger users.

Airtime is a real-time social co-watching platform that allows users to share and watch content together while interacting via live video, voice and text. Unlike platforms that primarily host pre-recorded content, Airtime's core experience involves live, real-time interaction with other users — including strangers in public rooms — which creates immediate and direct contact risks for children using the platform.
🎬
Public Watch Party Rooms Open to Any User on the Platform
Airtime's primary feature allows users to create or join public rooms where they watch videos or listen to music together in real time. These rooms are discoverable and joinable by any user on the platform, meaning that a child can enter a room shared with adults they do not know — and adults can join rooms that children are hosting. The live, real-time nature of these interactions means that inappropriate behaviour, contact or content can occur within seconds of a child joining or creating a room, with no pre-moderation in place to prevent it.
📹
Live Video and Voice Chat With Other Users in Real Time
Within watch party rooms, users can enable live video and voice chat, allowing all participants to see and hear each other in real time. This means that a child in a public room may be visible on live video to adult strangers — and those adults may be visible to the child. Live video interactions are not recorded or moderated in real time, and the spontaneous nature of room-based interactions means that inappropriate contact can occur very quickly in an environment where children feel they are simply watching videos with others.
💬
Text Chat and Direct Messaging Between Platform Users
Airtime includes text chat within rooms as well as direct messaging between users who have interacted on the platform. Direct messages allow any user who has been in the same room as a child to initiate a private conversation — creating the same direct contact risk as any messaging platform, but with the added context that the initial point of contact was a shared watch party room that the child may have joined for entirely innocent reasons. There is no parental visibility into these direct conversations by default.
🎵
Music and Video Content Shared by Other Users Without Pre-Moderation
Content within Airtime rooms is shared by users from external platforms and is not pre-screened for age-appropriateness before being played in a shared room. A child joining a watch party room may be exposed to adult-themed, violent or otherwise inappropriate video content that the room host has chosen to share. Because rooms are live and real-time, a child may be exposed to this content before they or a parent has any opportunity to review it.
🌐
Discoverable Public Rooms With No Meaningful Age Verification
Airtime's public rooms are discoverable through the platform's browse and search features, and any user can join any public room regardless of age. Account creation on Airtime requires only a date of birth with no identity verification, meaning that children can create accounts and access all platform features — including public rooms, live video and direct messaging — without any effective age check. The platform's age gate provides no meaningful protection against underage access to its full feature set.

Key risks from unrestricted use of Airtime, including live video contact with strangers, exposure to unmoderated content, the absence of meaningful age verification and the platform's design features that make it particularly easy for adults to initiate contact with children.

📹

🎭

A child can continue to use Airtime's full feature set — including public rooms, live video and direct messaging — through any browser on any device with an internet connection. 👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Airtime May Look Like a Harmless Entertainment App — Its Live Interaction Features Create Real Risk
Airtime's marketing and interface centre on the idea of watching videos and listening to music together with friends — an activity that sounds entirely benign and is genuinely how many younger users think of the platform. However, the same architecture that enables watching YouTube videos with a friend also enables a child to be in a live video room with adult strangers within moments of opening the app. The entertainment framing of the platform is one of its primary risk factors, because it reduces the likelihood that either a child or a parent will recognise it as a platform that enables direct, real-time contact with unknown adults.
2
The Live Video Format Makes Airtime Particularly Risky Compared to Text-Based Platforms
Many parents are familiar with the risks of text-based messaging platforms and monitor or restrict them accordingly. Live video contact carries additional risks that go beyond text — a child's appearance, location details visible in a home environment, and emotional reactions are all visible to strangers in a live video room in ways that text communication does not allow. Live video interactions are also not logged or recorded, making it impossible for parents to review what occurred during a session even if they become aware that an interaction took place.
3
Adults Seeking Contact With Children May Use Airtime's Rooms as a First Point of Contact
The public room structure of Airtime — where any user can join any open room — provides adults seeking contact with children a low-effort, low-suspicion way to begin an interaction. Joining a child's watch party room, or being in a room that a child joins, creates an immediate shared context that can be used to initiate conversation in a way that feels natural and unthreatening to the child. Child safety practitioners have identified this kind of entertainment-led contact initiation as a common pattern in online grooming cases. Parents should be aware that a platform that appears to be about entertainment may be being used for contact purposes.
4
Restricting Airtime Across All Devices Is the Most Reliable Safeguard for Younger Children
For children below their mid-teens, restricting Airtime entirely across all devices and browsers is the most reliable safeguard. The platform's live video and public room features carry risks that are difficult to mitigate through conversation alone, and the absence of any parental monitoring tools within the platform means that supervised use is not a practical alternative to restriction for younger children. For older teenagers, parents may choose to discuss the platform's risks and set clear household rules about which features are appropriate to use — but this conversation should be accompanied by iLimits controls that provide usage alerts and visibility, not permission granted in the absence of any technical safeguard.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Practical steps to protect your child from the risks associated with live video platforms and real-time stranger contact, and to build the kind of open conversation that keeps children safer online across every platform they use.

💬
Talk With Your Child About the Risks of Live Video With Strangers
An honest conversation about why live video with strangers is different from — and riskier than — text messaging is one of the most useful things a parent can do to protect a child who uses platforms like Airtime. Children may understand that they should not talk to strangers in person, but may not recognise that the same principle applies to live video rooms on entertainment platforms. Explaining clearly that adults in a watch party room are still strangers, that a live video call gives someone access to what your child looks and sounds like in real time, and that it is always safe to leave a room or conversation that feels uncomfortable will help your child make better decisions both on Airtime and on other platforms they use.
🌱
Help Your Child Understand What Makes a Platform Safe for Shared Watching
There are safe ways for children to enjoy co-watching and shared entertainment experiences online — platforms designed specifically for known-contact groups, or features within platforms they already use that allow them to watch content with friends they know in real life. Helping your child identify and prefer these options — rather than open-room platforms like Airtime that mix strangers into the experience — gives them a positive alternative rather than simply a restriction. A child who understands why one platform is safer than another is better equipped to evaluate new platforms they encounter in the future.
🔒
Apply iLimits Restrictions Across Every Device Your Child Has Access To
Apply iLimits restrictions for Airtime across every device in your household that your child uses or could access — including shared family tablets, secondary devices and any device they use regularly outside the home. Because Airtime is accessible via browser as well as app, ensure that browser-level restrictions are in place alongside app-level controls. Review your restrictions periodically, particularly when your child acquires a new device or begins using a new network. The combination of technical controls through iLimits and ongoing conversation about online safety gives your family the most complete and durable protection available.
