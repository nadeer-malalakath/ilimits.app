Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps for ProtonVPN — including the ecosystem account check that is specific to Proton users, how to engage with privacy-interested teenagers accurately, and why category-level blocking remains the most complete approach.

🔗
If You Use ProtonMail or Proton Services, Audit Which Devices Have Your Account Credentials Saved
If you use ProtonMail, Proton Drive or any other Proton service, the first practical step is reviewing which devices have your Proton account credentials saved. Your Proton account login grants access to ProtonVPN — and if that login is saved in a browser, a password manager, or an app on a device your child uses regularly, your child may already have access to ProtonVPN through your account without any new installation having taken place. Check every device your child uses regularly — including shared family computers and tablets — for saved Proton credentials. On any device where your child is likely to have access to your saved login, consider signing out of Proton services and changing your password. Applying iLimits restrictions to the device addresses the installation path; managing your account credentials addresses the ecosystem access path that iLimits cannot block directly.
💬
Engage With the Privacy Interest Honestly — the Concern Is the Network Bypass, Not Proton's Mission
Proton AG is a company built around privacy as a genuine value — it is associated with press freedom, digital rights advocacy and resistance to surveillance. A teenager interested in these topics who has found ProtonVPN will likely know this context and will not respond well to a conversation that treats ProtonVPN as if it were a suspect or untrustworthy product. The accurate and most productive framing: Proton's mission is legitimate and its products are among the most trustworthy in their categories; the reason ProtonVPN is restricted on their device is specifically about what any VPN does to the household's network filtering, not about Proton itself. Acknowledging the privacy interest as legitimate — while being clear that a household with children in it is not the same context as a journalist in a repressive country — gives the conversation a foundation that a privacy-interested teenager can engage with seriously.
🔒
Apply Category-Level VPN Restrictions, Enable Family Link Approval, and Separate Your Proton Account From Shared Devices
Apply iLimits restrictions at the VPN and proxy category level — this covers ProtonVPN alongside all equivalent alternatives and remains effective as the category evolves. Enable Google Family Link's app installation approval requirement to catch any new VPN installation before it succeeds. Separately, take the account management step specific to Proton: ensure your Proton account credentials are not saved on any device your child uses regularly, and consider whether your child needs access to Proton services for any legitimate purpose — if they use ProtonMail for their own email, they have their own Proton account, and that account is separate from yours. The combination of device-level restrictions, Family Link installation approval and active management of your own Proton account credentials gives the most complete coverage of the routes through which ProtonVPN might be accessed on a child's device.
