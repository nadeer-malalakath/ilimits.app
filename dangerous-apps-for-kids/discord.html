📱 Section 1

What This Platform Does

A clear picture of Discord — a widely used voice, video and text communication platform that hosts millions of public and private communities called servers, many of which are entirely unmoderated and accessible to any registered user regardless of age.

💬
Discord Is a Communication Platform Built Around Communities — Many of Which Have No Moderation or Age Restrictions
Discord is a real-time communication platform that allows users to join or create communities called servers, where members communicate through text channels, voice calls, video calls and direct messages. While Discord has legitimate and popular uses for gaming communities, friend groups and professional teams, the platform's open architecture means that any user — regardless of age — can join public servers that may contain adult content, violent imagery, extremist discussion or contact with strangers who are adults. Discord's minimum age requirement of 13 is not enforced through any meaningful verification, and many adult-oriented servers are accessible to anyone who accepts an age-confirmation prompt.

Discord is a real-time communication platform that allows users to join or create text, voice and video communities called servers. Widely popular among gamers and young people, it also hosts communities with unmoderated content and unrestricted adult-to-child contact, which poses significant risks for children who use it without parental awareness or oversight.
🏰
Public Servers With No Age Restrictions or Meaningful Moderation
Discord hosts millions of public servers covering virtually every topic, many of which are operated by private individuals with no moderation standards, no age verification and no oversight by Discord itself beyond a reporting mechanism. A child can join public servers containing adult content, violent imagery, hate speech or discussions of self-harm simply by accepting a confirmation prompt or clicking an invite link. Server owners can designate channels as NSFW (Not Safe for Work), but access to these channels is gated only by a self-declaration of adult age — a single click that any child can complete.
💌
Direct Messaging Between Any Users on the Platform
Discord allows any two users who share a server to send direct private messages to each other, creating a direct and unmonitored communication channel between a child and any adult who happens to be in the same server. This makes Discord a platform that has been used in documented cases of grooming, with adults using shared server membership as an initial point of contact before moving to private direct messages. Parents have no visibility into direct message conversations unless they have access to the child's device.
🎙️
Voice and Video Calls With Other Users, Including Strangers
Discord's voice and video features allow users to join open voice channels in servers, where they can hear and speak with all other users present — including adults they do not know. Children using Discord in shared gaming communities may routinely be in voice channels with adults who are strangers to them and their families. Voice and video conversations leave no text record and are not logged or monitored by Discord, making this form of communication particularly difficult for parents to oversee.
📂
File and Media Sharing Across Channels and Direct Messages
Discord allows users to share files, images, videos and links freely in both server channels and direct messages. Explicit images, videos and links to adult content can be shared in direct messages or in server channels that are not designated as NSFW, and there is no content scanning that would prevent the delivery of such material to a child's device. Children can also be sent unsolicited files or images through direct messages from any user they share a server with.
🔗
Invite Links That Bypass All Parental Discovery
Discord servers are accessible through invite links that can be shared anywhere — social media, gaming platforms, other messaging apps or in person. A child can join a server through an invite link without that server appearing in any discoverable directory, making it possible for a child to be a member of communities that a parent has no knowledge of and no way to discover without directly inspecting the child's Discord account. This makes Discord particularly difficult to monitor through observation alone.

Why It May Be Risky for Children

Key risks from unrestricted use of Discord, including contact with strangers, exposure to harmful communities, the absence of meaningful age verification and the platform's particular use in documented online safety incidents involving children.

👤
Anonymous Contact With Adult Strangers
Children on Discord can be contacted directly by adults who share any public server with them — creating immediate, private communication access between a child and an unknown adult with no parental visibility
🔞
Adult and Explicit Content Accessible in Servers
Many public servers contain NSFW channels or other adult material accessible to any user — including children — who accepts a self-declaration age prompt requiring no verification whatsoever
🎭
Grooming and Exploitation Risk
Discord has been cited in numerous safeguarding cases as a platform used by adults to initiate contact with children through shared servers before moving to private direct messages and exploitative conversations
💀
Harmful Community Content
Discord hosts communities that discuss self-harm, eating disorders, extremist ideologies and other harmful topics — many of which are accessible through public invite links without any age gate or safeguard
🌑
No Parental Visibility Into Conversations
Discord provides no parental monitoring tools — parents cannot see server memberships, direct messages, voice conversations or file transfers without having direct access to the child's device or account
🔒
Age Requirement Not Enforced
Discord requires users to be 13 or older, but account creation requires only a date of birth entry with no identity verification — meaning children of any age can create accounts and access the platform freely
🟡
Discord Carries a Medium-to-High Risk Rating Depending on Usage The platform's risks depend heavily on which servers a child joins and whether they engage in direct messaging with strangers. However, because parents have no visibility into these activities by default, and because the platform's architecture makes contact with adult strangers straightforward, Discord requires active parental oversight for any child user — not passive permission.
📡
Discord Is Accessible on Every Device — App Removal Alone Is Not Sufficient
Discord is available as a desktop application, a mobile app and a fully functional web version accessible through any browser. Removing the Discord app from a child's phone does not prevent access to the platform — a child can continue using Discord through any browser on any device. Effective restriction requires controls applied at both the app level and the browser level, across every device the child uses. iLimits provides controls that address this broader access vector, ensuring that restriction is applied consistently regardless of how the child attempts to reach the platform.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Discord Is Very Popular With Children and Teenagers — Familiarity Does Not Mean Safety
Discord is one of the most popular communication platforms among young people, and many children use it to stay in contact with school friends and gaming communities in ways that are entirely benign. However, the platform's popularity and legitimate uses can create a false sense that it is a safe platform for children. The same features that make Discord useful for friend groups — open servers, easy invite links, direct messaging — are the features that create the risk of contact with strangers, exposure to adult communities and access to harmful content. A child using Discord with school friends may also, on the same app, be in servers with adult strangers and communities a parent would not approve of.
2
Parents Cannot See What Happens on Discord Without Direct Access to the Child's Account
Discord does not offer parental accounts, family monitoring modes or activity reports available to a parent. A child's server memberships, direct messages, voice conversations and shared files are entirely private by default — a parent has no way to review any of this activity without physically accessing the child's device and logging into the child's account. This absence of transparency is one of the most significant risk factors for younger users, and means that parental trust in what a child is doing on Discord cannot be verified without technical controls or account-level access.
3
Discord Has Been Used in Documented Cases of Online Grooming of Children
Law enforcement agencies and child safety organisations in multiple countries have documented cases in which Discord was used by adults to initiate contact with children, build trust through shared gaming or community interests, and then progress to exploitative private direct message conversations. This does not mean that Discord is inherently dangerous or that every child using it is at risk — but it does mean that the risk is real and that the platform's architecture makes it particularly suited to the initial stages of grooming. Parents should be aware of this risk and consider whether their child has the maturity and awareness to navigate it safely.
4
A Conversation About Safe Discord Use May Be More Appropriate Than Outright Blocking for Older Teenagers
For some families, particularly those with older teenagers, blocking Discord entirely may not be the right approach — especially if the child uses it to communicate with known friends. In these cases, an honest conversation about which servers are appropriate, why direct messaging with strangers is unsafe, and what to do if something concerning happens online may be a more practical strategy than restriction alone. iLimits can be used alongside these conversations to provide alerts when the app is used rather than blocking it outright, giving parents visibility without removing access entirely.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Practical steps to manage your child's use of Discord safely — whether you choose to restrict access entirely or to allow supervised use with clear boundaries and open conversation.

💬
Have an Open Conversation About Who Your Child Talks to Online
Talking openly with your child about Discord — what servers they are in, who they talk to and why they use it — is one of the most effective safeguards available to parents. Children who feel they can share their online activities with a parent, rather than hiding them, are better protected than those who feel they must keep their digital lives entirely private. This conversation does not need to be confrontational — asking about what communities your child is part of, what they enjoy about the platform and whether anyone has ever made them uncomfortable online creates the openness that makes a child more likely to come to you if something concerning happens.
🔐
Review Your Child's Discord Account Together and Set Boundaries on Server Membership
If your child uses Discord, ask to review their account together — not as a punitive measure but as a normal part of family digital safety. Check which servers they are members of, whether any channels are marked NSFW, and whether they have direct message conversations with people they do not know in real life. Setting a clear household rule that Discord should only be used for communication with known friends, and that joining new public servers requires a parent's awareness, gives your child a framework for safer use rather than a blanket restriction that may encourage concealment.
🛡️
Use iLimits to Set Boundaries That Match Your Child's Age and Maturity
Use iLimits to apply restrictions that are proportionate to your child's age and the level of trust you have established around their online behaviour. For younger children, blocking Discord entirely across all devices and browsers is the most straightforward approach. For older teenagers, consider using iLimits to set time limits, receive usage alerts or monitor access patterns rather than blocking outright — this gives you visibility without removing a platform that may be genuinely useful for their social life. Review your settings periodically as your child gets older and as your family's conversations about online safety develop.
