📱 Section 1

What This App Does

A clear and fair picture of Psiphon — an open-source circumvention tool originally developed to help people in censored regions access the open internet, which on a child's device functions primarily as a means of bypassing network-level parental controls and content filters.

🔓
Psiphon Was Built for Press Freedom and Anti-Censorship — On a Child's Device It Bypasses Every Network-Level Parental Control You Have Set Up
Psiphon is a free, open-source VPN and proxy tool developed by Psiphon Inc., a Canadian company with a genuine mission of supporting access to information in regions where the internet is heavily censored. It is used by journalists, activists and ordinary citizens in countries where governments block access to news, social media and communication tools. On a child's device in a family home, the same technology routes internet traffic through Psiphon's external servers, bypassing any network-level content filtering or monitoring a parent has put in place — DNS-based filters, router restrictions and network monitoring tools all become ineffective for traffic routed through Psiphon.
⚖️
Psiphon Is Flagged for What It Does on a Child's Device — Not for Its Global Mission
Psiphon's work supporting press freedom and internet access in authoritarian contexts is genuinely important. It is flagged in iLimits not because the app is harmful in itself, but because the capability it provides — routing traffic through external servers to bypass network-level filtering — directly undermines parental controls in a family home. The technology does not distinguish between a government firewall and a parent's content filter. On a child's device, the practical effect is the same.

Psiphon is a free VPN and circumvention tool that routes internet traffic through external servers to bypass network-level restrictions. Developed for anti-censorship purposes, it functions on a child's device as a way to bypass parental content filters, network monitoring and DNS-based restrictions.
🌐
Routes All Internet Traffic Through External Servers, Bypassing Network Controls
Psiphon works by creating an encrypted tunnel between the device and Psiphon's external servers, through which all internet traffic is routed. Any network-level restriction — a router's content filter, a DNS-based blocking system, a household network monitoring tool — operates on traffic as it leaves the device for the local network. Traffic routed through Psiphon bypasses these controls before it reaches the point where filtering is applied, making every network-level parental control ineffective for as long as Psiphon is active and connected.
🔐
Encrypts Traffic — Making Content Invisible to Monitoring Tools
Beyond bypassing content filters, Psiphon encrypts the traffic it carries — meaning that network monitoring tools that log which websites a device visits, or which services it connects to, cannot inspect traffic routed through Psiphon. A parent using a router-level monitoring tool to review their child's browsing activity will see only a connection to Psiphon's servers, not the actual destinations the child visited or the content they accessed during the session. This removes network-level monitoring as a safety tool for any period when Psiphon is active.
🚪
Provides Access to Content Blocked by Network-Level Filters
Any content that a parent has blocked through a network-level filter — whether that is a DNS-based content category block, a specific website restriction, or a broader content filtering system applied at the router — becomes accessible to a child using Psiphon. The bypass is not selective: all network-level restrictions applied at the household network level are circumvented simultaneously for any content a child accesses while Psiphon is connected. Parental controls applied at the device level or through specific apps are not affected by Psiphon, but all network-level controls are.
📶
Connects Automatically and Can Run in the Background
Psiphon can be configured to connect automatically when the device detects a restricted network, or to run persistently in the background. A child who has set up Psiphon may have it active at all times without needing to manually activate it for each session. This means the bypass of network controls may be ongoing rather than occasional — operating silently in the background across all of the child's internet activity, not just for specific sessions they have deliberately chosen to route through a VPN.
📱
Available on Google Play — No Sideloading Required
Unlike many of the other tools flagged in iLimits, Psiphon is available through the Google Play Store and can be installed without any sideloading or "install unknown sources" permission. This means it can be installed on a device with Family Link active — though installing it requires a parent's approval if Family Link's app installation approval setting is configured. A child attempting to install Psiphon on a Family Link managed device will trigger an approval request; however, if Family Link is not set up or approval is not required, the app is straightforward to find and install through normal channels.

What Parents Should Know

1
Psiphon Is a Legitimate Anti-Censorship Tool Used by Adults in Repressive Contexts — Its Risk on a Child's Device Is About What It Bypasses, Not What It Contains
Psiphon is not malware, spyware or a piracy tool. It is a well-regarded, independently funded open-source project with a genuine global purpose. Parents who find Psiphon on a child's device should understand that the concern is entirely about what the tool does to their parental control infrastructure, not about Psiphon's own content or purpose. A child who has installed Psiphon has found and deployed a tool specifically capable of bypassing network-level restrictions — which suggests a level of deliberate effort that itself warrants a conversation. The question to ask is not primarily about Psiphon but about what content or platform the child was trying to access that prompted them to look for a bypass tool.
2
Parents Who Rely Heavily on Network-Level Controls Are Most Exposed to the Risk Psiphon Presents
Psiphon is specifically effective against network-level filtering — tools like DNS-based content blockers, router-based category filters, and household network monitoring systems. If a parent's digital safety setup relies primarily on these network-level tools, Psiphon can render them completely ineffective for any period it is active. Parents who combine network-level controls with device-level controls — such as those iLimits provides — are less vulnerable, because device-level controls operate independently of network routing and are not bypassed by VPN traffic. Understanding this layering is useful for assessing how significant the risk Psiphon presents actually is in the context of your specific setup.
3
Finding Psiphon on a Child's Device Is a Signal That They Were Looking for a Way Around Something
Unlike apps that a child might install without fully understanding their implications — cloning tools, third-party stores — Psiphon is specifically sought by people who know they want to bypass a network restriction. A child who has found, downloaded and set up Psiphon was looking for a way to access something they believed was blocked. This makes the conversation about why Psiphon is on the device as important as the decision about whether to block it. Understanding what the child was trying to access — and why they felt they needed to bypass the controls rather than ask — is more useful than simply removing the app and reinforcing restrictions without addressing the underlying situation.
4
Psiphon Is on Google Play — Family Link's App Installation Approval Is the First Line of Defence
Because Psiphon is available through the Google Play Store, it can be found and installed through normal channels without any sideloading. On a device where Family Link is configured to require parental approval for all app installations, a child's attempt to install Psiphon will trigger an approval request — giving a parent the opportunity to deny the installation and have the conversation before the tool is ever installed. This is one of the most practical arguments for ensuring Family Link's app installation approval is enabled and active, as it addresses the risk at the point of installation rather than after the fact. On devices where Family Link is not configured or approval is not required, the path to installing Psiphon is straightforward.

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps for addressing Psiphon — including the most important conversation to have, how to layer device and network controls effectively, and how to use Family Link's app approval as an early warning system.

💬
Focus the Conversation on What They Were Trying to Access — Not Just the Tool
The most productive conversation about Psiphon starts with a question rather than a statement: what were they trying to reach that they believed was blocked? A child who has taken the deliberate step of finding and installing a circumvention tool was motivated by something specific — a platform, a piece of content, a community. Understanding what that was gives you far more useful information than a conversation focused solely on the tool itself. Approach this with genuine curiosity rather than immediate consequences, because a child who feels they can tell you what they were looking for is more likely to come to you in future rather than escalating their circumvention methods. Address both the tool and the underlying motivation: why the restriction exists, and whether there is a way to address the underlying need within appropriate boundaries.
🔧
Layer Device-Level and Network-Level Controls — Don't Rely on Either Alone
Psiphon's ability to bypass network-level controls is a reminder that no single layer of parental control is complete on its own. Network-level filters are valuable but can be bypassed by VPN tools; device-level controls operate independently and are not bypassed by VPN routing, but can be circumvented if a child changes devices or accesses a different network. The most resilient approach combines both: iLimits at the device level to block circumvention tools before they can be used, and network-level controls as an additional layer for devices and traffic that fall outside device-level management. Review your current setup with this layering in mind, and ensure that neither layer is being relied upon as the sole protection.

Apply iLimits restrictions to block Psiphon and similar VPN and circumvention tools across all of your child's devices. Additionally, ensure Google Family Link is configured to require parental approval for all app installations on your child's device — this is the most direct way to catch an attempt to install Psiphon before it succeeds, because the app is available on Google Play and will trigger an approval request if Family Link is set up correctly. A denied approval request is also a useful signal: it tells you that your child was looking for a bypass tool, which opens the conversation described above, before the tool is ever installed. Combining iLimits restrictions with an active Family Link approval requirement addresses Psiphon at both the installation and the execution stage.
🔐

The Tool Tells You Something Important — Listen to What It Says

Finding Psiphon on a child's device is one of the more informative signals a parent can receive — it tells you that your child knew a restriction was in place, knew roughly how it worked, and went looking for a way around it. That is useful information. iLimits gives you the tools to block the circumvention at the device level and to receive the alert before the bypass succeeds. What you do with the information — the conversation about what they were reaching for and why — is where the most durable protection actually comes from.
