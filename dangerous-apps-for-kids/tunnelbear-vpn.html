Routes All Traffic Through External Servers — Bypassing Network-Level Controls
TunnelBear works by creating an encrypted tunnel between the device and its servers, routing all internet traffic through that tunnel. Any network-level parental control — a DNS content filter, a router category block, a household network monitoring system — operates on traffic as it leaves the local network. Traffic routed through TunnelBear bypasses all of these controls before filtering can be applied, making every network-level restriction in place ineffective for any session in which TunnelBear is connected. This is a structural feature of how VPN technology works, not a specific feature of TunnelBear.
🔐
Encrypts Traffic — Removing Browsing Visibility From Network Monitoring Tools
TunnelBear encrypts the traffic it carries, meaning network monitoring tools that log website visits or app connections cannot inspect the content of the tunnelled traffic. A parent reviewing router logs or a household monitoring tool will see only a connection to TunnelBear's servers — not the actual destinations visited or content accessed during the session. Any period when TunnelBear is active is a period during which network-level monitoring provides no useful record of the child's actual internet activity.
✅
Independent Security Audits and Transparent Privacy Policy
TunnelBear is one of a small number of consumer VPN services that undergoes independent third-party security audits and publishes the results — a genuine differentiator in a market where most free consumer VPN products make privacy claims without independent verification. Its privacy policy is written clearly and makes specific commitments about data not being logged or sold. These characteristics make TunnelBear a meaningfully more trustworthy VPN provider than most free alternatives, which is relevant context for parents assessing the data practice dimension of the risk.
