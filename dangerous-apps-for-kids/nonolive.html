Gaming Livestreams With Public Chat and No Pre-Moderation
Nonolive was originally positioned primarily as a gaming livestream platform, hosting live broadcasts of games including League of Legends, PUBG, Arena of Valor and Minecraft. Gaming streams are open to the public and accompanied by unmoderated live chat rooms in which any registered viewer can post messages, share images and interact directly with the broadcaster and other viewers in real time. 🎁
Virtual Gift System That Enables Children to Send Real Money to Strangers
Nonolive operates a virtual gifting system in which viewers purchase virtual coins and use them to send digital gifts to broadcasters during live streams. These gifts have real monetary value — broadcasters can convert accumulated gifts into cash. The system is designed to gamify financial interaction with broadcasters and to encourage viewers to spend money to gain recognition from their favourite streamers. Children with access to a parent's stored payment credentials, or with access to gift cards or digital payment methods, can send real money to adult strangers on the platform without any additional verification being required.

Real-Time Chat Rooms Connecting Children With Unknown Adults Globally
Every Nonolive stream is accompanied by a live public chat room in which any registered user can communicate with the broadcaster and with other viewers in real time. Chat is global, entirely public and unmoderated before publication — meaning any comment, including inappropriate, sexual, predatory or harmful content, can reach a child viewer instantly. The platform also supports private messaging features that allow individual users to communicate one-to-one outside the broadcast context. Both the public chat room and private messaging represent direct channels through which unknown adults worldwide can contact a child using the platform.

Nonolive supports one-click mobile broadcasting, meaning any user — including a child — can begin streaming live video of themselves to a global public audience in seconds from a smartphone. A child who creates an account on Nonolive can broadcast their face, their location, their home environment and their personal details to an unlimited number of unknown viewers worldwide, with no parental oversight, no broadcast delay and no moderation check before the stream goes live.
🔓
Age Gate Reliant on Self-Declaration With No Meaningful Verification
Despite carrying a 17+ age rating on the Apple App Store and a High Maturity content rating on Google Play, Nonolive's account creation process relies on a self-declaration of birth date that any child can falsify in seconds. The platform's official age rating represents the minimum age it is considered suitable for — not a barrier that prevents younger users from accessing it.

Gaming Platforms Are Not Safe by Default — Content and Interaction Features Create Significant Risks Regardless of the Gaming Context
Parents may associate Nonolive primarily with gaming — a category they may perceive as lower risk than explicit entertainment platforms — but the platform's gaming content exists alongside and on the same interface as explicitly sexualised "beauty anchor" streams, with no separation between them. 2
The Platform's Virtual Gifting System Is Designed to Encourage Ongoing Financial Engagement — Stored Payment Credentials on Shared Devices Create Direct Financial Risk
Nonolive's coin and virtual gift system is a core part of the platform's design, and it is specifically engineered to make sending money to broadcasters feel like a social and status-conferring activity rather than a financial transaction. Children who become engaged with the platform's community may feel social pressure to send gifts to broadcasters they follow. Any stored payment method on a shared device — including saved card details, Apple Pay, Google Pay or stored app store credentials — provides a child with the means to make real financial transfers through the platform without requiring any additional authentication that would notify a parent.
3
Live Broadcasting Features Mean Children Can Expose Themselves to Global Audiences Without Realising the Full Implications
Nonolive's one-click mobile broadcasting feature enables any user to begin streaming live video to a global public audience instantly. Children and teenagers may not fully appreciate that going live on the platform means their face, voice, location environment and any identifying information visible in their room or their stream is immediately available to an unlimited number of unknown viewers worldwide, including individuals who may record the stream for distribution on other platforms. Anything broadcast live cannot be retracted once it has been viewed or recorded by others. Parents should ensure children understand that Nonolive's broadcasting feature is not equivalent to a private video call.
4
Comprehensive Restriction Across All Devices and Browsers Is More Effective Than Removing the App From a Single Device
Because Nonolive is accessible through any web browser as well as through its dedicated apps, removing the app from a child's phone provides only partial protection. Children can reach the platform through a phone browser, a tablet, a shared family computer or any other internet-connected device. Effective restriction requires controls applied consistently at the browser and network level across all of a child's devices — not only on the device from which the app was removed. Parents who have removed the app may believe access is blocked when in fact the platform remains fully reachable through a browser on the same device. iLimits provides the controls necessary to address this.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps to protect your child from the risks of unmoderated live streaming platforms and build a foundation of open conversation about safe online interaction, the permanence of live broadcast content, and responsible digital habits.

💬
Talk Openly With Children About the Risks of Real-Time Interaction With Strangers Online
Children who use live streaming platforms need to understand that the people they are interacting with in real-time chat rooms are unknown adults, not peers, and that the apparent friendliness of a chat interaction does not make it safe. Age-appropriate conversation about why unknown adults might seek out contact with children online — including direct discussion of online grooming — equips children to recognise warning signs and to come to a parent if something feels wrong. Equally important is ensuring children understand that anything they broadcast live can be recorded by viewers and shared anywhere on the internet, permanently, without their knowledge or consent. These conversations do not need to be alarming — they should be calm, honest, and framed around giving the child the knowledge they need to stay safe.
🌱
Establish Clear Household Rules About Live Streaming, Broadcasting and Online Financial Transactions
Set explicit household norms around live streaming: which platforms are permitted, whether broadcasting live is something your child is allowed to do, and what kinds of chat interaction are appropriate. The virtual gifting features on platforms like Nonolive deserve specific conversation — children should understand that sending gifts sends real money to strangers, and that the platform is designed to make this feel normal and desirable. Review all payment methods stored on devices your child uses and consider removing or password-protecting them to prevent unauthorised purchases. Children who are given a clear understanding of the financial reality behind virtual gifting are significantly less likely to make uninformed purchases through these systems.
🔒
Use Parental Control Tools to Restrict Access to Live Streaming Platforms Across All Devices and Browsers
Apply iLimits restrictions consistently across every device your child can access — including tablets, secondary phones and shared family computers — and ensure that browser-level restrictions are in place on all of these devices, not only app-level controls. Because Nonolive is accessible through any web browser, app removal alone is not sufficient. Review stored payment credentials on all shared devices and consider enabling password requirements on any payment method accessible through an app store. Combine technical controls with the open conversations described above — restriction and communication together provide the most complete and durable protection available to your family.
