What This App Does

A clear picture of Sendit — an anonymous Q&A add-on that integrates directly with Snapchat, allowing a child to post a question sticker to their Snapchat Story and receive completely anonymous responses from anyone who sees it. Sendit's integration with Snapchat's existing social graph is what makes it distinctively risky: anonymous harassment and stranger contact arrive through the platform the child already trusts, among people they already know.

📩
Sendit Brings Anonymous Messaging Into Snapchat — Routing Untraceable Messages Through a Platform and Friend Network the Child Already Trusts
Sendit is a Snapchat-integrated anonymous Q&A tool that allows a user to add an anonymous question sticker to their Snapchat Story. Anyone who views the Story can submit an anonymous message, which Sendit collects and delivers to the account holder. The critical distinction from standalone anonymous Q&A platforms like Tellonym is that Sendit operates within Snapchat's existing social ecosystem — the anonymous messages appear to come from within the trusted friend network the child has already built on Snapchat, making them harder to be appropriately sceptical of. The child's entire Snapchat Story audience — including people added as "friends" who may be acquaintances or near-strangers — gains anonymous messaging access automatically when a Sendit sticker is posted.

Anonymous Messages Appear to Come From Within a Trusted Social Network
Because Sendit operates through Snapchat, anonymous messages appear to the recipient as if they come from within their established friend and follower network rather than from unknown strangers on a separate platform. This is the key distinction between Sendit and standalone anonymous Q&A platforms: a child who receives an anonymous message through Sendit will likely assume it comes from someone in their Snapchat circle — a classmate, a friend, a peer they know. This assumption makes the child less likely to treat the message with the scepticism appropriate to contact from a complete stranger, and more likely to engage with, respond to, or be emotionally affected by its content as if it comes from someone in their social world.
👥
The Entire Story Audience Gains Anonymous Messaging Access Instantly
When a child posts a Sendit sticker to their Snapchat Story, every account that views their Story can submit an anonymous message — without any additional steps, without creating a Sendit account, and without any identity verification. A child's Snapchat Story audience may include people they have added as friends who are more acquaintance or near-stranger than close connection, people who have been added through friend-of-friend chains, and potentially adults who have found their way into the child's social circle through any number of Snapchat-specific discovery mechanisms. All of them gain instant anonymous access the moment the Sendit sticker goes live in the Story.
😔
Creates the Same Anonymous Harassment Channel as Tellonym — Inside a Platform Already on the Device
The anonymous harassment risk of Sendit is structurally identical to Tellonym's: the sender is completely anonymous, the recipient is named and identified, and harmful messages cannot be traced back to their source. The key difference is that Sendit operates through a platform — Snapchat — that is almost certainly already on the child's device, already integrated into their social life, and already trusted. A child who would not seek out a standalone anonymous Q&A platform may adopt Sendit simply because it appears as an option within the Snapchat ecosystem they already use every day, without fully understanding what anonymous messaging access they are opening to their Story audience.
💰
Sendit Charges Fees to Reveal Sender Hints — Creating a Financial Incentive Around Harmful Content
Sendit offers a paid feature that provides partial hints about who sent an anonymous message — not full identity disclosure, but approximate clues such as the sender's age range, general location, or astrological sign. This creates a financial incentive structure around harmful content: a child who has received a cruel or upsetting anonymous message may be motivated to pay for a hint to understand who sent it, with no guarantee the hint is accurate or meaningful. The payment mechanism is a specific concern because it can lead children to spend money in distressing circumstances — paying for information that may not resolve the distress, while providing Sendit with revenue generated by the negative emotional impact of anonymous harassment.

Sendit's Integration With Snapchat Is the Feature That Makes It More Dangerous Than a Standalone Anonymous Q&A App — Not Less
It might seem that an add-on to an existing, monitored platform is less risky than a standalone anonymous social app — because the parent is already aware of the child's Snapchat use, already potentially monitoring their Snapchat activity, and the child is not going elsewhere for their social interaction. In practice, the integration makes Sendit more dangerous for the specific harm of anonymous harassment. A child who receives a cruel anonymous message through a dedicated platform like Tellonym knows it has come from someone with access to a separate platform. A child who receives the same message through Sendit experiences it as if it comes from within their Snapchat friend network — from someone they know, someone in their social world — which makes the emotional impact more direct, more personal, and more difficult to distance from.
👨‍👩‍👧 Section 3

What Parents Should Know

1
Sendit May Be on a Child's Device Even if a Parent Is Already Monitoring Their Snapchat
Parents who monitor their child's Snapchat usage — reviewing snaps, checking stories, or using Snapchat's own family tools — may not be aware of Sendit as a separate app that integrates with Snapchat. Sendit's anonymous messages are delivered through the Sendit app itself rather than appearing as standard Snapchat messages — a parent reviewing Snapchat conversations will not see Sendit content in the Snapchat inbox. The Sendit app must be checked separately, and its presence in the installed app list must be identified specifically. A child who understands that a parent reviews their Snapchat messages may use Sendit precisely because anonymous messages are delivered to the Sendit app rather than appearing in the standard Snapchat message history a parent might review.
2
The Paid Sender Hint Feature Can Lead Children to Spend Money While Experiencing Emotional Distress
Sendit's paid sender hint feature is specifically designed to be purchased in the context of receiving an anonymous message — the feature is presented as a way to find out who sent a message the child has already received and is already emotionally responding to. A child who has received a cruel, confusing, or emotionally affecting anonymous message is in precisely the state of distress most likely to lead to an impulsive in-app purchase — they want to know who sent it, and Sendit offers a paid pathway to partial information. Parents whose children have Sendit installed should check whether any in-app purchases have been made, as these may indicate the child has been receiving messages distressing enough to motivate spending money on hints. Any in-app purchase history in Sendit is significant context for the conversation about what the child has been experiencing through the app.
3
Children Adopt Sendit Through Peer Group Social Dynamics Within Snapchat Itself
Because Sendit appears as a sticker option within Snapchat's Story creation interface, children encounter it in the context of their existing Snapchat social activity rather than as a separate app to be deliberately sought out. A child who sees a classmate's Snapchat Story with a Sendit sticker, responds to it, and is then prompted to create their own, can adopt Sendit through a sequence of steps that feels like ordinary Snapchat interaction rather than a deliberate choice to install an anonymous messaging platform. This means parents cannot rely on the deliberateness of installation as a signal that a child has actively sought out an anonymous Q&A tool — the adoption pathway through Snapchat is specifically designed to feel seamless and social rather than intentional.
4
Restricting Sendit Does Not Affect Snapchat — They Are Separate Apps
Sendit is a separate application from Snapchat — it is installed independently, operates as a distinct app on the device, and can be restricted or removed without affecting the child's Snapchat account or access. Restricting or deleting Sendit through iLimits or directly from the device removes the anonymous messaging add-on while leaving Snapchat intact. This is an important clarification for children who may resist the restriction on the grounds that they do not want to lose access to Snapchat — the restriction of Sendit is targeted specifically at the anonymous messaging add-on, not at the underlying social platform. If a Sendit sticker has already been posted to a Snapchat Story, removing the Sendit app does not automatically remove the sticker from the Story — the Story may need to be deleted or allowed to expire for the anonymous message collection link associated with it to become inactive.

💡 Section 6

Guidance for Parents

Three practical steps that reflect Sendit's Snapchat-integrated nature — checking the Sendit inbox separately from Snapchat messages, addressing the paid sender-hint feature, and explaining the restriction without conflating Sendit with Snapchat itself.

💬
Be Clear That Restricting Sendit Is Not Restricting Snapchat — And Explain What Sendit Specifically Enables
One of the most important things to establish in the conversation about Sendit is that restricting the Sendit app is not the same as restricting Snapchat. A child who hears that Sendit is being blocked may immediately assume that their Snapchat access is also under threat, and may resist the conversation on that basis. Making clear at the outset that Sendit is a separate app that adds anonymous messaging to Snapchat — and that restricting it removes only the anonymous add-on, not the social platform — removes that concern and makes the conversation about the specific risk Sendit creates rather than about Snapchat use generally. The conversation can then focus specifically on what anonymous messaging means: anyone who sees a Snapchat Story with a Sendit sticker can send an anonymous message with no ability for the recipient to know who it came from or prevent further messages from the same person.
🔎
Check Sendit's Inbox, Any In-App Purchases, and Active Story Stickers Before Removing the App
Open the Sendit app separately from Snapchat and review the anonymous messages that have been received in its inbox — these will not appear in the child's standard Snapchat message history and are only visible within the Sendit app itself. Check for any in-app purchases related to sender hints — these indicate messages distressing enough to motivate spending, and are important context for understanding what the child has been experiencing. Also check the child's active Snapchat Stories for any Sendit sticker that may currently be collecting anonymous messages — a live Sendit sticker on an active Story continues to allow anonymous submissions until the Story expires or is deleted, even after the Sendit app is removed from the device. Deleting the active Story or waiting for it to expire closes that particular anonymous access point. After reviewing all of this, remove the Sendit app and apply the iLimits restriction to prevent reinstallation.
🔒
Block Sendit Through iLimits and Review Snapchat's Own Privacy Settings as a Complementary Step
Use iLimits to block Sendit on the child's device. As a complementary step, review the child's Snapchat privacy settings to ensure their Story audience is appropriately limited — Snapchat allows Story visibility to be restricted to close friends rather than all friends or public. A child whose Story is visible to a large and loosely connected Snapchat audience is more exposed to anonymous messaging from near-strangers than one whose Story is limited to close connections, regardless of whether Sendit is in use. Snapchat's Family Centre feature also provides parents with some visibility into their child's Snapchat contacts and activity, and is worth enabling as a complementary layer of oversight for the platform that Sendit integrates with. The combination of Sendit restriction through iLimits and tighter Snapchat privacy settings provides the most complete protection against the specific harm that Sendit introduces into the Snapchat ecosystem.
